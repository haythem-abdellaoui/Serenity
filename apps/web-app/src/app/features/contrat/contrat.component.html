1
Position & Scope of Services

The Practitioner is engaged as a Licensed Psychologist and shall provide professional psychological services exclusively through Serenity's secure digital platform. All consultations and treatments are conducted online, in compliance with Tunisian telemedicine regulations. The Practitioner's responsibilities shall include, but are not limited to:

  • Conducting comprehensive psychological assessments and diagnostic evaluations via video consultation for adult and adolescent patients registered on the Serenity platform
  • Providing individual online psychotherapy using evidence-based modalities including CBT, DBT, ACT, and trauma-informed approaches through Serenity's encrypted video sessions
  • Facilitating scheduled online group therapy sessions as coordinated by the Platform's clinical team
  • Preparing and maintaining complete, accurate, and timely clinical documentation within Serenity's digital health records system
  • Participating in weekly virtual multidisciplinary team meetings and online peer supervision sessions
  • Providing crisis assessment and coordinating referrals for emergency in-person interventions in accordance with Platform protocols
  • Maintaining a minimum availability of 20 online consultation hours per week as displayed on the Practitioner's Serenity profile calendar
  • Responding to patient messages through the Platform's secure messaging system within 24 hours on working days

3
Online Availability & Digital Workspace

As Serenity is a fully remote, digital-first platform, the Practitioner works entirely online from any location within Tunisia. The Practitioner shall maintain a consistent and visible availability schedule on the Platform to ensure continuity of patient care.

  • The Practitioner must keep their Serenity availability calendar up to date at all times, with a minimum of 20 bookable hours per week visible to patients
  • All sessions must be conducted via Serenity's encrypted, HIPAA-equivalent video consultation system — no third-party video tools are permitted
  • The Practitioner is responsible for ensuring a stable internet connection, professional background, adequate lighting, and a private, confidential environment during all consultations
  • The Practitioner must log all session notes into Serenity's digital health records system within 24 hours of each consultation
  • Weekend and evening session slots are strongly encouraged to maximize patient accessibility across Tunisia
  • The Practitioner may not redirect or treat Serenity-referred patients through external channels or personal private practice accounts
4
Professional Credentials & Compliance

The Practitioner warrants and represents throughout the term of this Agreement:

  • Holds an active, unrestricted license to practice psychology issued by the relevant Tunisian authority (Ordre des Médecins de Tunisie or equivalent body)
  • Maintains a valid university degree in psychology (Doctorate/Master's level) recognized by Tunisian higher education authorities
  • Remains free of any disciplinary actions, sanctions, or malpractice judgments that have not been disclosed to the Platform in writing
  • Complies with all applicable Tunisian data protection regulations (loi organique n° 2004-63), telemedicine guidelines issued by the Tunisian Ministry of Health, and the deontological code governing psychologists in Tunisia
  • Provides the Platform with immediate written notice of any change in licensure status, credential limitations, or legal proceedings
5
Confidentiality & Data Privacy

The Practitioner acknowledges that in the course of their engagement, they will have access to highly sensitive patient health data and proprietary platform information belonging to Serenity. The Practitioner agrees to:

  • Maintain strict confidentiality of all patient records, session notes, and clinical communications in full compliance with Tunisian data protection law (loi organique n° 2004-63 relative à la protection des données à caractère personnel)
  • Access patient data solely through Serenity's official platform interface and only on a need-to-know basis for direct patient care
  • Never download, copy, or store patient data on personal devices or external storage without explicit written authorization from the Platform
  • Refrain from disclosing any proprietary clinical protocols, platform algorithms, pricing structures, or operational systems of Serenity, both during and for a period of two (2) years following the termination of this Agreement
  • Report any actual or suspected data breach or unauthorized access to the Platform's Data Protection Officer within 24 hours of discovery
6
Termination of Agreement

Notice Period: Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause by providing no fewer than thirty (30) days written notice to the other party via the Platform's official communication channel or registered email. The Platform reserves the right to suspend the Practitioner's account access during the notice period while ensuring all active patients are properly transferred.

This Agreement may be terminated immediately by the Platform, without notice, in the event of: (a) license revocation or suspension; (b) a material breach of patient confidentiality or data protection obligations; (c) clinical misconduct or gross negligence during online sessions; (d) a violation of the deontological code resulting in formal disciplinary action by competent Tunisian authorities; or (e) conduct that, in the Platform's reasonable determination, poses a risk to patient safety or Serenity's reputation.

7
Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation

For a period of twelve (12) months following the conclusion of this Agreement, the Practitioner agrees not to establish or join any directly competing Tunisian online mental health platform offering equivalent digital psychology services. The Practitioner further agrees not to solicit, directly or indirectly through any digital channel, any patient who received services through the Serenity platform during the term of this Agreement.
